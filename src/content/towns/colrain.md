Colrain is a hilltowns community in northern Franklin County. Like most rural Franklin County towns, Colrain has no municipal sewer system, and all properties depend on private septic systems regulated under **Massachusetts Title 5** (310 CMR 15.000).

## Health Services

Colrain's Board of Health operates through the **Franklin Regional Council of Governments (FRCOG) Cooperative Public Health Service (CPHS)** — the shared health services network serving 13 towns in Franklin County. The CPHS provides professional public health staff to handle permitting, inspection oversight, and regulatory compliance for the Colrain Board of Health.

## What Rules Apply

As of our last verification, Colrain does not have local septic regulations that exceed the Title 5 state standard. All septic work — whether new installation, repair, or upgrade — must comply with:

- Massachusetts Title 5 design and construction standards
- Required inspections at the time of property transfer
- Repair and replacement obligations for systems that fail inspection
- Setback requirements as defined under Title 5

## Hilltown Site Conditions

Colrain's hilly terrain and variable soils can present challenges for conventional septic system design. Properties with steep slopes, shallow depth to bedrock, or high groundwater may require alternative or innovative system technologies. A licensed soil evaluator should assess your site before you proceed with any design or purchase decisions.

## Permitting Process

Applications in Colrain are processed through CPHS:

1. Contact FRCOG/CPHS to start the permitting process
2. Arrange a site evaluation with a licensed professional
3. Obtain a system design from a licensed engineer
4. Submit the application through CPHS for Board of Health review and approval

## Contact Information

As of our last verification, specific contact details for the Colrain Board of Health through CPHS were not on file. Please contact FRCOG's Greenfield office or Colrain Town Hall to get current contact information.
