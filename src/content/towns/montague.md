# Septic System Regulations in Montague, MA

Montague is a multi-village community in Franklin County with a population of approximately 8,600. It is one of the larger towns in the county. Montague has **partial municipal sewer coverage** — some areas of town are served by the municipal sewer system, while other areas (particularly more rural villages) rely on private septic systems.

## Municipal Sewer vs. Private Septic

Montague's sewer system serves parts of the town, primarily in denser village areas. Properties connected to municipal sewer are not subject to Title 5 or private septic permitting. However, a significant portion of Montague — especially rural parcels outside the sewer service area — relies on private onsite septic systems.

**To determine whether your property is on sewer or septic**, contact the Montague Department of Public Works or the Board of Health.

## Health Services

Montague maintains its own independent **Board of Health** with staff appropriate to a community of its size.

## What Rules Apply for Septic Properties

As of our last verification, Montague does not have local septic regulations beyond the Massachusetts Title 5 minimum for properties on private septic. These properties must comply with:

- Title 5 design and installation requirements
- Mandatory inspection at property transfer
- Setback requirements from wells, wetlands, and water bodies
- Repair or replacement requirements for failed systems

## Permitting Process

For properties on private septic in Montague:

1. Contact the Montague Board of Health to confirm your property status and begin your application
2. Hire a licensed site evaluator and engineer
3. Submit your system design for Board of Health review
4. Obtain approval before beginning construction
5. Schedule a final inspection upon completion
