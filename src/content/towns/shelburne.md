Shelburne is a small Franklin County community located along the Deerfield River. The town has no municipal sewer system, and all properties rely on private onsite septic systems regulated under **Massachusetts Title 5** (310 CMR 15.000). Shelburne maintains its own independent Board of Health.

## Health Services

Shelburne has an independent **Board of Health** responsible for septic permitting, inspections, and health regulation enforcement within the town.

## What Rules Apply

As of our last verification, Shelburne does not have local septic regulations that exceed the Title 5 state standard. All septic work — new installations, repairs, and upgrades — must comply with Title 5 requirements:

- Soil evaluation and percolation testing before design
- Licensed engineer system design
- Board of Health review and approval before construction
- Required inspection at property transfer
- Repair or replacement requirements for systems that fail inspection

## Deerfield River Corridor

Shelburne's location along the Deerfield River means that properties near the river, associated tributaries, or wetlands may have septic system siting constraints. Title 5 establishes setbacks from surface water that will affect system placement for riverside properties. Homeowners should discuss water resource proximity with their engineer and the Board of Health early in the design process.

## Permitting Process

1. Contact the Shelburne Board of Health to discuss your project
2. Hire a licensed site evaluator and engineer
3. Prepare a system design meeting Title 5 standards
4. Submit the design to the Board of Health for review and approval
5. Begin construction only after receiving written approval
6. Schedule a final inspection upon completion
