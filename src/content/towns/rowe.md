# Septic System Regulations in Rowe, MA

Rowe is one of the smallest communities in Franklin County, with a population of approximately 380 people. Located in the remote northwestern corner of the county near the Hoosac Range, Rowe has no municipal sewer system, and all properties rely on private onsite septic systems regulated under **Massachusetts Title 5** (310 CMR 15.000).

## Health Services

Rowe's Board of Health services are provided through the **Franklin Regional Council of Governments (FRCOG) Cooperative Public Health Service (CPHS)**. The CPHS model is essential for a community of Rowe's size, providing professional public health staffing and permitting infrastructure that would otherwise be unavailable.

## What Rules Apply

As of our last verification, Rowe does not have local septic regulations beyond the Title 5 state minimum. All septic work must comply with standard Title 5 requirements:

- Soil evaluation and percolation testing
- Licensed engineer design
- Board of Health (through CPHS) review and approval
- Required inspection at property transfer
- Repair and replacement obligations for failed systems

## Remote Location Considerations

Rowe's remote location in the Hoosac mountain region presents logistical challenges for septic work:
- Finding contractors and engineers willing to travel to Rowe may require extra effort and may come with travel surcharges
- Rocky, thin soils and steep terrain are common, making some conventional system types infeasible
- Permitting through CPHS in Greenfield is generally efficient but requires coordination across distance

If you own undeveloped land in Rowe, obtain a soil evaluation before committing to development plans.

## Permitting Process

1. Contact FRCOG/CPHS in Greenfield to initiate the application
2. Identify qualified contractors experienced in remote hilltown work
3. Complete soil evaluation and system design
4. Submit through CPHS for Board of Health review

## Contact Information

Rowe has no meaningful online presence. All BOH matters are handled through FRCOG/CPHS in Greenfield.

> **Note for Rowe property owners:** Remoteness adds cost and complexity to septic work. Budget for contractor travel and challenging site conditions.
