Leyden is a very small rural community in northern Franklin County near the Vermont border. With a population of approximately 700, the town has no municipal sewer system, and all properties use private onsite septic systems regulated under **Massachusetts Title 5** (310 CMR 15.000). Despite its small size, Leyden maintains an independent Board of Health.

## Health Services

Leyden has its own independent **Board of Health**, separate from the CPHS shared services network. However, given the town's very small size, the Board may operate with limited meeting frequency or volunteer-based staffing. Response times and availability may differ from larger towns.

## What Rules Apply

As of our last verification, Leyden does not have local septic regulations beyond the Title 5 state standard. All septic work must comply with Title 5:

- Licensed professional soil evaluation and perc testing
- System design prepared by a licensed engineer
- Board of Health review and approval before construction
- Required inspection at property transfer
- Repair or replacement of systems that fail inspection

## Small Town Considerations

Because Leyden has an independent but small Board of Health, it is especially important to contact the Board early in your project to understand meeting schedules and any local procedures. Board meetings may be infrequent, and delays in getting on the agenda could affect your project timeline.

## Permitting Process

1. Contact the Leyden Board of Health to discuss your project and learn meeting schedules
2. Hire a licensed site evaluator and engineer
3. Submit your system design application to the Board
4. Obtain approval before beginning work
5. Schedule a final inspection upon completion

Leyden has minimal online presence. Contact Leyden Town Hall for current Board of Health contact information and meeting schedules.
